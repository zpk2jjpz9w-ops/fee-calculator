üìê High Water Mark Formula

How High Water Mark is Calculated

New HWM = AUM + Gross Return ‚àí Management Fee ‚àí Performance Fee

The High Water Mark tracks the net asset value after all fees are deducted.
Performance fees are only charged when NAV exceeds the previous HWM.

üí° Why HWM Matters for Revenue Projections

  • Down years: No performance fees collected (only management fees)
  • Recovery years: No performance fees until fund exceeds previous peak
  • Impact: Even 1 down year can mean 2-4 years of reduced performance fee revenue

üí° Key Insights for HWM Adjustment

Recommended HWM Adjustment Settings

  • Best case (Millennium-like): ~5% reduction - For funds with exceptional risk management and quantitative strategies
  • Realistic case: ~15-20% reduction - For well-managed diversified funds
  • Conservative case: ~25% reduction - Your current default, appropriate for most scenarios
  • Pessimistic case: ~30-40% reduction - For volatile strategies or newer funds

üìå Bottom Line

The 25% default HWM adjustment in your revenue prognosis tool is well-calibrated for a realistic scenario. Elite quantitative funds like Renaissance and Millennium achieve near-perfect consistency (4% impact), but most top-tier funds experience 3-4 negative years per 25-year period with 1-2 year recovery periods (12-16% impact).

For a new fund, using 20-25% is prudent. Once you establish a track record, you can adjust based on actual performance.
